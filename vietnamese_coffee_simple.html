Vietnamese Coffee

Learn about Vietnamese coffee culture

What is Vietnamese Coffee?

Vietnamese coffee is a popular drink in Vietnam. It is made with dark roasted coffee beans and condensed milk. The coffee is brewed using a special metal filter called a phin.

Types of Vietnamese Coffee

There are several popular types of Vietnamese coffee:

  • Ca Phe Sua Da - Iced coffee with condensed milk
  • Ca Phe Sua Nong - Hot coffee with condensed milk
  • Ca Phe Den - Black coffee without milk

How to Make Vietnamese Coffee

Vietnamese coffee is made using a phin filter. You place the filter on top of a cup, add coffee grounds, and hot water. The coffee slowly drips down. This can take about 5 minutes. Then you mix it with condensed milk and enjoy.

Coffee Culture in Vietnam

In Vietnam, coffee is more than just a drink. Coffee shops are places where people meet friends, discuss business, and relax. Vietnamese people love to drink coffee throughout the day.
